Get a ready‑to‑use foundation pack, generate a circuit pack from a requirement, or build your own pack from exact MPNs. Every pack follows the same deterministic naming and description rules.

Pack A: Foundation Packs (miniature design essentials)

Curated ‘miniature design essentials’ built to the naming/description rules — the baseline library you can reuse across projects.

  • 0201 / 0402 passive sets (E24 / E96 starter ranges)
  • Miniature ferrite bead and TVS protection sets
  • Common ultra‑miniature B2B connector sets

Pack B: Circuit Packs (subcircuit kits)

Search a requirement like ‘1A DCDC with 5V input’ and receive 5 candidate IC options. Upon selection, it will present:

  • the main part + all supporting parts required to build the subcircuit (decoupling, passives, protection as needed)
  • consistent names/descriptions, and wiring intent notes

Pack C: Build‑your‑own pack (exact MPN list)

Paste your exact MPNs one by one that you need:

  • We generate deterministic footprint names and structured descriptions.
  • We can also suggest supporting parts from our database to complete buildable blocks (optional).
  • You add to Content Cart list → we deliver the pack.

The standard is the product

Ultra‑dense design fails when every engineer names parts differently. SketchCAD enforces a deterministic footprint naming and description system so content stays consistent across teams, projects, and contractors.

  • Footprint names encode maximum physical dimensions whenever available.
  • Descriptions stay compact and practical (≤256 characters).
  • Ambiguity is explicitly flagged for review.

Example: CAP_0402_1uF_10V_X5R_1.0x0.5x0.5mm · Description kept compact, technical, and parser-friendly.

What the central catalog covers (and what contractors handle)

Our central Content Cart catalog focuses on parts that show up repeatedly in miniature designs and can be standardized safely.

In scope (central packs)

  • Passives & protection (RES/CAP/IND, ferrites, TVS, ESD)
  • Common IC packages (QFN/DFN/SOT/SOD/SOP etc.)
  • Ultra‑miniature connectors commonly used in small devices (B2B/FPC sets)
  • Other miniature‑friendly components as the catalog grows

Out of scope (contractors)

  • Push buttons and specialized electromechanical UI parts
  • Non‑chip LEDs, displays, bespoke mechanical parts
  • Anything not typical for miniature design foundations
