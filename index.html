Beyond-IPC BodyMax discipline for ultra-miniature AI hardware

AI changes hardware constraints — not just firmware.

Classic IoT often meant sense, send, and process in the cloud. AI-connected devices increasingly need local intelligence, longer recording windows, higher-density storage, and smaller power-constrained electronics.

  • Edge processing for latency, privacy, bandwidth, and power.
  • Local recording and NV storage for high-data-rate sensors.
  • Higher power density from compute, rails, sensors, and thermal limits.
  • Miniature movement for micro-robotics and compact mechatronics.

To ship these devices, teams need mobile-class engineering discipline — not ad-hoc libraries and inconsistent footprints.

Beyond IPC means predictable space.

IPC-aware generators are widely available. They are useful for general manufacturability, but ultra-miniature products need a stricter layer: an envelope contract for every component. SketchCAD specialises in that layer.

Maximum body size is the truth. Encode it, enforce it, and your density becomes predictable.

  • Predictive layout density: parts cannot silently grow beyond the chosen envelope.
  • Fewer mechanical surprises: height, origin, rotation, and package assumptions stay consistent.
  • Faster iteration: less rework from footprint drift and inconsistent conventions.
  • Mobile-style repeatability: packs can be reused across products without library chaos.

If you want standard IPC-style output, many tools already exist. SketchCAD focuses on the foundation that enables smartphone-grade densification: BodyMax discipline.

Content Cart Packs

Three ways to build your foundation: start from curated essentials, generate a complete subcircuit kit, or build a pack from your exact MPN list. All packs follow the same BodyMax and deterministic naming discipline.

Pack A: Foundation Packs

Curated miniature design essentials built to the naming and description rules — the baseline library you can reuse across projects.

  • 0201 / 0402 passive sets (E24 / E96 starter ranges).
  • Miniature ferrite bead and TVS protection sets.
  • Common ultra-miniature B2B connector sets.

Pack B: Circuit Packs

Search a requirement like ‘1A DCDC with 5V input’ and receive 5 candidate IC options. Upon selection, it will present:

  • the main part + all supporting parts required to build the subcircuit, including decoupling, passives, protection, and other needed support parts.
  • consistent footprint names, structured descriptions, and wiring intent notes.

Pack C: Build-your-own pack

Paste your exact MPNs one by one that you need:

  • We generate deterministic footprint names and structured descriptions.
  • We can also suggest supporting parts from our database to complete buildable blocks, if you choose.
  • You add parts to the Content Cart list → we deliver the pack.

What we standardise centrally — and what contractors handle

Our central catalog focuses on parts that repeatedly appear in ultra-miniature electronics and can be standardised safely under BodyMax rules. We do not try to standardise everything under the sun.

Central catalog focuses on

  • miniature passives and protection.
  • common IC package families used in small devices.
  • ultra-miniature B2B and compact connector sets.

Contractors handle everything else

  • push buttons and specialised electromechanical UI parts.

  • custom engineering tasks where standardisation is unsafe.
